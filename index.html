When leaders call

When execution can't keep up with what's changing.

The execution gap change exposes

The strategy is fine. What's missing is the ability to execute it at the pace the business now moves — decisions slow, meetings multiply, and the people closest to the work end up figuring it out as they go, in real time.

AI got adopted before it got governed

Every team picked its own tool. Nobody owns the workflow, the risk, or the return — so the investment shows activity, not ROI.

A merger or acquisition scrambled everything

Systems, culture, and customer commitments all have to move together, and someone has to actually run that, not just plan it.

Capacity is walking out the door

Customer outcomes depend on a few people's memory and goodwill instead of a repeatable system — so when they burn out or leave, the capacity leaves with them.

The PMO tracks work instead of driving it

Reporting exists. Decisions still don't get faster or clearer because of it.

Representative career experience

Recognizable organizations. Complex operating environments.

No quota. Just an expectation to grow the account.

Owned revenue-base growth across a $100M+ portfolio with no formal sales quota — just a standing expectation to grow every account a minimum 5% year over year. Sustained 98-99% customer retention and 13-15% annual growth across Fortune 500, Federal, Healthcare, and Financial Services accounts.

An $80M "PMO" that everyone called broken — it wasn't a PMO

Nearly 300 people carried a Project Manager title, but most were doing delivery and logistics work wearing a PM label — which is why everything sold off that portfolio was compromised from the start, and why people called the PMO itself broken. It wasn't. Diagnosed it by reading every SOW against real timesheets and time-to-task studies, then rebuilt the roles with honest titles and real KPIs for the Services and Operations work actually being done — the fix that would have moved CSAT, margin, and pricing, not more PM process.

What was sold didn't match what could be delivered

SOWs and amendments were sold in ways delivery couldn't fulfill profitably. Rewrote and re-solutioned them end-to-end so the commitments became deliverable and margin-positive.

2 people to 400+, across 27 global locations

Scaled a global data center services field delivery program from a two-person team to more than 400 professionals — a blend of FTEs and contingent/contract staffing partners — supporting roughly $1B in annual program revenue without breaking service quality.
